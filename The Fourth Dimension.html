The Fourth Dimension

Most people perceive only three dimensions: length, breadth, and height. But the fourth dimension—time—adds motion and change to everything we see. In physics, this is called spacetime, where the past, present, and future coexist in a single continuum. Artists and writers have long imagined traveling through this dimension, unlocking portals to alternate realities. From Einstein’s theory of relativity to science fiction movies like Interstellar, the fourth dimension represents humanity’s desire to understand existence beyond the visible. It reminds us that reality is not just what we see, but also what we can imagine.
