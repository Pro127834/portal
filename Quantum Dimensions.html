Quantum Dimensions

At the quantum level, the rules of reality change completely. Particles can exist in multiple places at once, vanish, or even teleport—a phenomenon called quantum entanglement. Scientists believe these effects might hint at extra dimensions beyond the visible three. The string theory suggests there could be 10 or even 11 dimensions folded into microscopic spaces. Though invisible, these dimensions could explain the deepest mysteries of the universe—from gravity to black holes.
